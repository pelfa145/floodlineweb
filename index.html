Before a Flood

  • Go-Bag Ready — Pack 3 days of food, clean water, flashlight, and extra batteries.
  • Secure Documents — Place birth certificates and IDs in a waterproof plastic pouch.
  • Know Your Route — Identify the quickest path from your Purok to the designated evacuation center.
  • Elevate Belongings — Move electronics and appliances to the second floor or higher platforms.
  • Check Drainage — Ensure small canals near your home are clear of debris to prevent clogging.

During a Flood

  • Power Down — Switch off the main electrical breaker and unplug all appliances.
  • Stay High — If trapped, move to the highest floor. Avoid attics without a roof exit.
  • Flash Floods — If you hear a roar or see water rising rapidly near the creek, move immediately.
  • No Wading — Do not walk through moving water; 6 inches is enough to knock an adult down.
  • Monitor Radio — Keep a battery-operated radio tuned to local emergency broadcasts.

After a Flood

  • Check Structure — Look for cracks or shifting in your home before entering.
  • Sanitize Everything — Assume flood water is contaminated; scrub walls and floors with bleach.
  • Water Safety — Boil water for at least 15 minutes before drinking or using for cooking.
  • Snake Awareness — Be careful of snakes or insects that may have sought refuge in your home.
  • Wait for 'All Clear' — Do not return home until MDRRMO officially declares the area safe.

General Flood Safety

What to Do:

  • Listen to the radio or television for the latest information and instructions.
  • Be aware of streams, drainage channels, and other areas known to flood suddenly.
  • If you must evacuate, secure your home and take your emergency kit.
  • Turn off utilities at the main switches or valves if instructed to do so.

What Not to Do:

  • Do not walk through moving water. Six inches of moving water can make you fall.
  • Do not drive into flooded areas. It's easy to be swept away.
  • Do not touch electrical equipment if you are wet or standing in water.
  • Do not eat any food that has come into contact with floodwater.
